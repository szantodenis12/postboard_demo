### For: Roland (Epic Digital Hub internal use)

**Status: BLOCKED — waiting for interview answers**

---

## Where We Are Now

We have solid strategic groundwork:
- The **mirror document** (what we see from outside)
- The **interview guide** (what we need from him) — PDF compiled and ready to send
- The **internal analysis** (our positioning hypothesis: "builder of autonomy")

But we're missing the most critical piece: **Marius's own answers**. Without those, anything we build — logo, website, messaging — is based on our hypothesis, not his truth.

---

## The "No Professional References" Constraint

Marius doesn't want to expose his current jobs and personal life publicly. This changes the game significantly. The internal analysis leans heavily on MAI, CISO, Babeș-Bolyai as credibility signals. If those are off the table publicly, the brand has to stand on:

- **What he knows** — cybersecurity, digital citizenship, resilience, integration
- **What he's built** — ROUA, Armonia, foundation programs (if he's OK naming those)
- **What he believes** — his worldview, philosophy, vision for Romania
- **How he thinks** — the pattern: building autonomy, not dependency

That's actually a stronger foundation for a personal brand. People follow thinkers, not job titles.

---

## Phased Plan

### Phase 1 — Now (this week)

1. Send him the PDF interview guide + mirror document
2. Give him 1-2 weeks to respond
3. These answers are the raw material for EVERYTHING else

### Phase 2 — Brand Foundation (after interview)

1. Write the **Brand Positioning Document** — core message, voice, values, story, pillars
2. Define the **visual identity direction** — before jumping to a logo, we need to know what the brand *feels* like. Calm authority? Bold reformer? Warm builder?
3. Name/wordmark decision — is it "George Șinca", "Marius Șinca", just "Șinca", or something else entirely?

### Phase 3 — Visual Identity + Website

1. **Logo** — probably a clean wordmark, given it's a personal brand. Minimal, authoritative, not corporate.
2. **Website** — expertise-led, content-first. Structure:

   - **Hero**: His name + one line that captures the "builder of autonomy" idea (validated through interview)
   - **Areas of thinking**: Digital citizenship, community resilience, education, integration — positioned as expertise domains, not work history
   - **Content hub**: Blog posts, podcast episodes, essays — this is where the real brand gets built over time
   - **About**: His story told through beliefs and impact, not CV. The interview answers will feed this directly.
   - **Contact/Speaking**: For invitations, media, collaboration

---

## Why We Don't Rush the Logo and Website

They'll be 10x better if we wait for interview answers. A logo designed around a validated positioning is an asset. A logo designed before you know who you're designing for is just a pretty shape.

The interview answers will tell us:
- Whether the "builder of autonomy" frame is right or needs adjustment
- What his origin story is (feeds the About page)
- What his voice actually sounds like when he's being honest (feeds the tone of everything)
- What expertise domains he wants to lead with (feeds site structure)
- What visual world fits him — we can't pick colors and type without knowing who he really is
